# Style: Editorial

**Best for**: narrative-led proposals, thought leadership, executive storytelling, anything where
the argument matters more than the data.

## Shape Language
Minimal use of hard-edged containers. Where boxes appear, corners are gently rounded (large radius,
~0.15–0.2× the box height) and borders are thin or absent — content is separated by white space and
type hierarchy, not by boxes and rules.

## Layout Grammar
- Generous margins — at least 1" on every side, more on title/section slides.
- Asymmetric layouts are welcome: a large pull-quote or headline can occupy two-thirds of a slide
  with a single supporting image or stat in the remaining third.
- One idea per slide is the default; when a slide must carry a list, cap it at 4 items.
- Section dividers use a full-bleed background (colour or image) with a single large headline —
  no logo/chrome competing for attention on these slides.

## Density
Low. This is the least dense style in the library on purpose — word count per slide should be low
enough that the slide works as a backdrop to spoken narration, not a document to be read silently.

## Colour Emphasis
`primary` dominates section dividers and backgrounds. `accent-1` is used sparingly, for a single
highlighted word or figure per slide (not for repeating chip/category systems — that's Bold
Diagram-First and Minimal Exec territory). Avoid using more than two accent colours in any one deck.

## Typography Scale
| Element | Size | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| Section divider headline | 40–48pt | Regular or Light |
| Slide headline | 28–32pt | Regular |
| Pull-quote / featured stat | 32–40pt | Regular, often italic |
| Supporting body | 14–16pt | Regular |
| Caption / attribution | 10pt | Regular, muted colour |

## Example Slides

1. **Section divider** — full-bleed `primary` background, no logo, single 44pt line of text
   left-aligned at vertical centre: *"The cost of doing nothing is no longer zero."* Slide number
   omitted.
2. **Narrative slide with pull-quote** — white background, a 36pt italic quote occupies the left
   two-thirds of the slide, attribution in 10pt muted text beneath it; right third holds a single
   supporting statistic in `accent-1` at 40pt with a one-line caption.
3. **Closing slide** — `primary` background, brand mark centred (from brand-core `assets/mark.svg`),
   a single line of text below it: *"Let's build what's next."* No bullet list, no agenda recap.
