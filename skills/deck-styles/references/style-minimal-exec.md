# Style: Minimal Exec

**Best for**: C-suite steering updates, board decks, one-decision-per-slide status reports — anything
read by people with 90 seconds per slide, not 5 minutes.

## Shape Language
Almost no containers at all. When a box is unavoidable (e.g. a single KPI callout), it's a plain
rectangle or borderless coloured panel — no shadows, no gradients, no decorative elements of any kind.

## Layout Grammar
- Strict single-column or single-focal-point layout — one headline number, one chart, or one
  decision statement per slide, centred or left-aligned with wide margins (1.25"+).
  Never split a Minimal Exec slide into more than two visual regions.
- No agenda slides with more than 5 items; no "thank you" filler slides.
  Every slide must answer "so what" in the first line the eye lands on.
- Status/RAG (red-amber-green) indicators, where used, are a single small dot or short colour bar —
  never a large coloured block competing with the headline.

## Density
The lowest information density of any style by design, deliberately at the cost of nuance — this
style is for decisions and direction, not detail. If the content resists this treatment, it belongs
in Technical Deep Dive or a supporting appendix, not forced onto a Minimal Exec slide.

## Colour Emphasis
`primary` and `secondary` only for backgrounds/text. A single accent colour communicates status per
slide (e.g. `accent-3` for "on track", a palette-appropriate warm tone for "at risk") — never more
than one status colour per slide, and never the full accent set at once (that's Bold Diagram-First).

## Typography Scale
| Element | Size | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| Headline stat / decision statement | 44–60pt | Bold |
| Supporting one-liner | 16–18pt | Regular |
| Status label | 12pt | Bold, uppercase, short (1–2 words) |
| Footer/source note | 9pt | Regular, muted |

## Example Slides

1. **Single KPI slide** — centred on the slide: "94%" at 60pt bold, directly beneath it in 16pt
   "of Phase 1 milestones delivered on schedule." Nothing else on the slide except a 9pt source
   footnote.
2. **Decision slide** — top third: a one-sentence decision statement at 32pt bold ("Recommend
   proceeding to Horizon 2 as scoped"); bottom two-thirds: three short supporting lines at 16pt,
   each with a small coloured status dot, no bullet symbols.
3. **RAG status slide** — a single row of 3–5 short labels (workstream names) each with one small
   coloured dot (green/amber/red) and a 4–6 word status line — no table, no borders, generous
   horizontal spacing between items.
